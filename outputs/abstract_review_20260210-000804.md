## Corrected Abstract
```
3D sand mould printing using binder jet technology is driving innovations in casting, enabling part consolidation, material and hazardous chemical optimization, and flexible, on-demand manufacturing near customers. The integration of artificial intelligence facilitates automated optimization of mould design, printing parameters, and solidification processes, which reduces labor and enhances efficiency. Achieving sustainable manufacturing requires overcoming the challenge of eliminating hazardous chemicals; research into alternative sand fusing methods and eco-friendly binders is recommended. Parts produced by 3D printed sand moulds can exhibit superior properties compared to traditional casting, due to the advanced geometry and process control achievable with additive manufacturing. Rather than competing with traditional casting, 3D sand mould printing enhances it, supporting complex or customized part production. This paper reviews two decades of research, highlighting the technological advancements, sustainability challenges, and industry paradigm shift that 3D sand printing has brought to casting design.
```
