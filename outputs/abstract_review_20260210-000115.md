## Corrected Abstract
```
Additive manufacturing through 3D sand mould printing using binder jet technology is transforming conventional casting practices. This process enables part consolidation, optimizes material and chemical usage, and supports flexible, on-demand production close to customers. The integration of artificial intelligence enhances automation by optimizing mould designs, process parameters, and solidification, reducing labour requirements and production time. To address environmental challenges, research is focusing on developing alternative technologies for sand particle fusion and selecting eco-friendly binders and consumables, aiming to minimize hazardous chemicals in industrial applications. Parts produced with 3D printed sand moulds can achieve superior properties compared to traditional castings, making this technology an enabler rather than a competitor to established methods. The paradigm shift in casting design results from innovative mould geometries made possible by additive manufacturing. This paper reviews the first twenty years of research, outlining technical advances and sustainability challenges in the development of 3D sand mould printing as a key innovation for modern manufacturing.
```
